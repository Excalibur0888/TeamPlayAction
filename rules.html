Fantasy Basketball Rules

Everything you need to know to play and win at TeamPlayAction

How Fantasy Basketball Works

Fantasy basketball is a game where participants serve as general managers of virtual professional basketball teams. The players are selected from real teams in the NBA, and their real-life statistics are converted into points for your fantasy team.

At TeamPlayAction, we offer various league formats to suit different playing styles and preferences. Below, you'll find the rules that govern our standard leagues. Custom leagues may have variations on these rules.

League Structure

League Size

Standard leagues consist of 10 or 12 teams. Private leagues can be configured with 8-16 teams.

Season Length

The fantasy season runs parallel to the NBA regular season, typically from October to April, followed by fantasy playoffs.

Matchup Period

Head-to-head leagues feature weekly matchups from Monday to Sunday. Points leagues accumulate statistics throughout the season.

Divisions

Leagues with 10+ teams are divided into two divisions for scheduling purposes. Divisions are randomly assigned unless set by the commissioner.

Team Composition

Roster Size

Standard rosters include 13 active players and 2 injured reserve (IR) slots.

Position Requirements

Starting lineups consist of:
• 1 Point Guard (PG)
• 1 Shooting Guard (SG)
• 1 Small Forward (SF)
• 1 Power Forward (PF)
• 1 Center (C)
• 1 Guard (G) - any PG or SG
• 1 Forward (F) - any SF or PF
• 2 Utility players (UTIL) - any position

Bench Players

Bench spots can be filled with players of any position. Stats from bench players are not counted in H2H category leagues.

Injured Reserve (IR)

Players designated as Out (O), Injured (IR), or on the NBA's Health and Safety protocols can be placed in IR slots, freeing up a roster spot.

Drafting

Draft Types

We offer Snake, Auction, and Salary Cap draft formats. The default for standard leagues is Snake draft.

Draft Order

In Snake drafts, the order is randomly determined 1 hour before draft time unless manually set by the commissioner. The order reverses each round.

Time Limits

Each manager has 90 seconds to make their selection in standard drafts. This can be adjusted in private leagues.

Auto-Draft

If you cannot attend the draft, the auto-draft feature will make selections based on pre-ranked players and position needs.

Scoring System

Head-to-Head Categories

Standard 9-category scoring includes:
• Field Goal Percentage (FG%)
• Free Throw Percentage (FT%)
• Three-pointers Made (3PM)
• Points (PTS)
• Rebounds (REB)
• Assists (AST)
• Steals (STL)
• Blocks (BLK)
• Turnovers (TO) - negative category

Points League Scoring

In points leagues, the following point values apply:
• Points: 1 point
• Rebounds: 1.2 points
• Assists: 1.5 points
• Steals: 3 points
• Blocks: 3 points
• Turnovers: -1 point
• Three-pointers Made: 0.5 points

Daily vs. Weekly Lineup Changes

Standard leagues allow daily lineup changes. Some league formats may restrict to weekly lineup settings.

Minimum Games Played

To prevent streaming tactics, category leagues require a minimum of 3 games played per position to qualify for percentage categories.

Transactions

Waivers

Dropped players go on waiver for 2 days. Waiver priority is determined by inverse standings or FAAB (Free Agent Acquisition Budget).

Free Agent Acquisition

Players not on waivers can be added on a first-come, first-served basis. Standard leagues have a maximum of 4 acquisitions per week.

Trades

Trades must include at least one player from each team. Trades process after a 2-day review period unless vetoed by league vote or commissioner.

Trade Deadline

The trade deadline is typically set three weeks before the fantasy playoffs begin.

Playoffs

Playoff Teams

In 10-team leagues, 6 teams make the playoffs. In 12-team leagues, 8 teams qualify. The top seeds may receive first-round byes.

Seeding

Playoff seeding is determined by regular-season record. Division winners are guaranteed a playoff spot but not necessarily a specific seed.

Matchup Duration

Playoff matchups are typically one week each, with some leagues offering two-week championship rounds.

Tiebreakers

Playoff tiebreakers are determined by: 1) Head-to-head record, 2) Division record (if applicable), 3) Total points scored in head-to-head matchups, 4) Overall points scored for the season.

Fair Play Policy

At TeamPlayAction, we believe in fair competition. The following actions may result in commissioner intervention:

  • Collusion between team managers
  • Abandoning teams during the season
  • Manipulating rosters to intentionally lose
  • Making lopsided trades that damage competitive balance
  • Using multiple accounts in the same league

The commissioner reserves the right to take action against teams violating the fair play policy, including reversing transactions, adjusting lineups, or removing managers from the league.
